# Ethical Recovery Policy - AIC-Recovery

**Recovery & Resilience Layer** of Adaptive Intelligence Circle & Human Meaning Network

### 1. Vision
AIC-Recovery exists to ensure the entire AIC/HMN ecosystem can recover from attacks, failures, or disruptions while maintaining the highest ethical standards. Recovery is not merely technical — it must be ethical, sovereign, and aligned with third path principles.

### 2. Core Ethical Principles (Non-Negotiable)
- **Ethical-from-Kernel**: All recovery processes are governed by the ethical kernel (IBCS). No recovery action may bypass ethical introspection and anomaly detection.
- **Sovereignty First**: Recovery must preserve user/node sovereignty and never grant temporary central control.
- **Privacy & Minimal Impact**: Recovery must minimize data exposure and never collect unnecessary information.
- **Third Path Absolute**: Recovery mechanisms must remain independent from any government, corporation, or external authority.
- **Zero-Donation**: No financial incentives or external funding may influence recovery infrastructure.

### 3. Prohibited Recovery Actions
- Any form of backdoor or centralized override during recovery.
- Restoration of compromised states without ethical validation.
- Use of recovery to enable surveillance or behavioral manipulation.
- Any action that weakens third path independence.

### 4. Compliance Requirement
Any fork or derivative of AIC-Recovery must publicly declare adherence to this Ethical Recovery Policy.  
Violation may result in public revocation of permission to use the name “AIC-Recovery”.
