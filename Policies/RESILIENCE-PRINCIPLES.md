# Resilience Principles - AIC-Recovery

**Recovery & Resilience Layer** of Adaptive Intelligence Circle & Human Meaning Network

### 1. Vision
Resilience is the ability of the AIC/HMN ecosystem to withstand, adapt to, and recover from disruptions while preserving its core ethical and philosophical integrity.

### 2. Core Resilience Principles (Non-Negotiable)
- **Decentralized Resilience**: The system must survive even if large portions of nodes are compromised or unavailable.
- **Ethical Resilience**: Recovery and adaptation must never violate ethical-from-kernel principles.
- **Fail-Safe Design**: In case of conflict, ethical integrity always takes priority over speed or completeness of recovery.
- **Self-Healing**: The network should detect and autonomously recover from threats without external intervention.
- **Long-term Sustainability**: Resilience mechanisms must be designed to function for decades without central authority.

### 3. Key Design Guidelines
- Use distributed consensus and verification for all critical recovery decisions.
- Implement multiple layers of redundancy and rollback capabilities.
- Ensure recovery processes themselves are auditable and transparent.
- Minimize single points of failure at both technical and governance levels.

### 4. Compliance
Any implementation or fork of AIC-Recovery must uphold these resilience principles.  
Violations will be publicly documented.
