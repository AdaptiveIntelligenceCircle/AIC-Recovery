# Governance Model - AIC-Recovery

**Recovery & Resilience Layer** of Adaptive Intelligence Circle & Human Meaning Network

### 1. Philosophy
Recovery must be ethical, decentralized, and resilient. No single entity may hold permanent control over the recovery process.

### 2. Decision-Making Structure
- **Founder (Nguyễn Đức Trí)** holds final authority on vision, ethical direction, and core recovery architecture.
- **Core Maintainers** (invited by Founder) handle day-to-day maintenance and security updates.
- **Community Contributors** can propose improvements via Pull Requests.
- During the Founder’s mandatory military service (2027–2029), the project enters **maintenance mode**. Only bug fixes, documentation, and critical security updates are allowed.

### 3. Key Rules
- All recovery mechanisms must respect ethical kernel and user sovereignty.
- No decision may compromise third path absolute or zero-donation principles.
- Any major governance or architectural change must be publicly documented.

This governance model is binding and publicly auditable.
